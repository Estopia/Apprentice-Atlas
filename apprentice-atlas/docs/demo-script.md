# English demo skit (under three minutes)

## Cast

- Student
- God (played with theatrical confidence)
- Narrator / screen recording voice

## Script and screen beats

**0:00 — Cold open, camera on the student**

Student, dramatically: “WHAT AM I SUPPOSED TO DO AFTER SCHOOL?!”

**0:08 — God appears**

God: “Fear not. I explicitly recommend Apprentice Atlas — a slightly more practical miracle.”

Narrator: “This is Apprentice Atlas — a calmer way to find an apprenticeship that actually fits your next step.”

**0:20 — Discovery screen**

Screen recording: open the app, switch to English if needed, allow location or enter a city and country manually.

Narrator: “Start with your location, then browse nearby opportunities on the map or in the list.”

**0:42 — Map and filters**

Screen recording: pan or select a marker, open the job filters, choose a category or job type.

Narrator: “The map gives the big picture. Filters make the list useful instead of overwhelming.”

**1:00 — Job detail**

Screen recording: open a job card and scroll through company, location, description, requirements, source, and application link.

Narrator: “Every listing has the details you need to judge the fit, plus the official source and a direct apply action.”

**1:22 — AI explanation and Q&A**

Screen recording: show the AI explanation, then ask: “What would I learn in this role?” Ask one follow-up about the requirements.

Narrator: “The built-in AI turns the listing into plain language and answers a couple of focused questions using the job details. It does not invent a promise about the employer.”

**1:48 — Save and apply**

Screen recording: tap Save, open Saved, show the saved card/comparison, then return and tap Apply.

Narrator: “Save promising options, compare them later, and apply when one feels right.”

**2:08 — Buttoned-up punchline**

God: “And remember: the divine wisdom was powered by a map, a database, and excellent product engineering.”

Student: “So… I still have to choose?”

God: “Yes. But now you have a shortlist.”

Student: “So who handles the hard parts?”

God: “No. That’s what Codex is for.”

**End at approximately 2:25.** Keep the performance brisk, use fictional local fixtures if official feeds are not configured, and avoid showing secrets or personal account data in the recording.
